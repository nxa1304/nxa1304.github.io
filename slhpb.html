SINH LÝ HỌC PHÊ BÌNH

Albert Thibaudet (1874-1936 ) was the most important literary critic of the interwar period, on the model of Sainte-Beuve and especially of Montaigne, "father of the critical spirit".

Student then friend of Bergson, classmate of Jarry and Herriot, admirer of Barrès, Maurras and Mistral, he was also close to Gide and Valéry, the interlocutor of Proust, the equal of Alain and Daniel Halévy , the rival of Julien Benda and the accomplice of Jean Paulhan. Philosopher, then historian and geographer, his wide curiosity, his benevolent versatility, the ingenuity of his comparisons and his familiar style gave a European audience to his column of La Nouvelle Revue française.

Month after month, from 1912 to his death, he dealt with all subjects, big and small, low and high, serious and funny, not only the last releases but also the classics, not to mention the films of Chaplin.
